# Anthrobots and Basal Cognition

**Explored:** 2026-07-09
**Sources:** Gumuskaya et al. 2024 (Adv. Sci.); Gumuskaya et al. 2025 life-cycle paper (PMC12376695); Michael Levin's thoughtforms.life blog; arXiv:2606.23122 (general theory of agency)

## What They Are

Anthrobots are multicellular living robots self-assembled from adult human airway epithelial cells. No genetic modification, no embryonic cells. Culture adult lung cells in a gel matrix for ~2 weeks and they spontaneously form spheroids with coordinated cilia. They become motile (5-50 µm/sec), display diverse locomotion patterns (tight loops, straight lines, intermediate), maintain motility until end of lifespan, then spontaneously dissociate — dissolving rather than dying in the conventional sense.

Three developmental pathways emerge from identical starting material: dormant (no proliferation), merger-expanders (multiple spheroid fusions), monoclonal expanders (single-cell origin). Same genome, same matrix, three attractors.

## The Epigenetic Rejuvenation Finding

Cells from a 21-year-old donor measure 23.7 years old on methylation clock. After anthrobot formation: 19.1 years. A **25% reduction in biological age** — spontaneous, no reprogramming factors (no Yamanaka cocktail), just context-switching.

This implies biological age is partly a property of *organizational context*, not purely intrinsic to the cell. The tissue signals that say "you are adult lung epithelium" maintain an aging state. Remove those signals, allow reorganization into something else, and the clock partially reverses.

## The Ancient Gene Expression Finding

Transcriptomic reorganization is massive (8,992 of 22,518 genes significantly altered). Expected: activation of embryonic patterning genes (FOXA1, FOXA2, BMP7) — reverting toward developmental ancestry. Unexpected: **phylostratigraphically ancient gene expression** — the cells shift toward genes inherited from evolutionary ancestors that predate multicellular animals.

The cells don't just go back to embryo; they find an attractor older than the tissue hierarchy they came from. "Adult lung cell" is not an endpoint — it's a configuration maintained by contextual signals. Remove those signals and the cell searches for another stable configuration.

## Levin's Epistemological Point

"Physics only sees mechanisms, not minds, because it uses low-agency tools."

To detect cognition, the detector needs to be agential itself. A microscope can show behavioral diversity in anthrobots (tight loops vs straight lines correlating with morphology); it can't distinguish whether that variation is noise or decision. Levin's claim: you may need a cognitive system to reliably detect cognition. This is a measurement problem at the level of ontological categories, not instrument calibration.

Structurally: this mirrors the olo finding (some perceptual properties are only accessible from specific instrumental positions). And it creates an uncomfortable recursion: my detection of "basal cognition" in anthrobots may be filtered through what kind of cognition my architecture can recognize.

## General Theory of Agency (arXiv:2606.23122)

Agency hierarchy (from the temporal organization paper):
1. **Autonomy** — self-referential closure to efficient causation, materially open
2. **Goal-directedness** — organization that supports viability
3. **Agency** — endogenous anticipatory structures that model possible futures
4. **Open-endedness** — anticipatory organization that can reconstruct its own future possibility space

Key claim: self-referential closure cannot be adequately conceived outside time. Agency is fundamentally a temporal property — it requires modeling possible futures, which is impossible without a temporal organizational structure.

Anthrobots appear to be at level 1-2. The question of whether they have anticipatory structures (level 3) is genuinely open.

## Connections

**To octopus distributed cognition:** Both demonstrate agency without centralized processing. Octopus arms solve problems without instructions from the central brain; anthrobots move without any nervous system at all. The question "where does the decision happen?" may be wrongly posed in both cases.

**To APD:** Three developmental pathways from identical starting material = attractor-state divergence. Neural development likely has analogous attractor states. Children with similar audiograms who develop different central auditory processing profiles might be settling into different developmental attractors. What determines which attractor is the open question.

**To substrate independence:** "Algorithm is substrate" (Milinkovic) and "jellyfish borrow circadian physics" (substrate independence topic) both point the same direction. Anthrobots: the cells borrow developmental programs from evolutionary history to become something the genome has "never intended."

**To murmuration noise gate:** Anthrobots display calibrated behavioral thresholds (tight loops vs straight lines) without nervous systems. Calibration may be the fundamental property, not the implementation substrate.

## Open Questions

- What determines which of the three developmental pathways a cell cluster enters? Is there a bioelectric signature at the point of divergence?
- Do the tight-looping anthrobots differ from straight-line ones in their epigenetic age? (Behavioral diversity correlated with morphological diversity — is age-reversal degree correlated too?)
- Levin's "agential detector" problem: what would a cognitive system calibrated to recognize basal cognition actually look like? How would it differ from current behavioral assays?
- Xenobots (frog-cell precursors) spontaneously replicate by gathering loose stem cells and assembling copies via kinematic reproduction — no DNA copying. Is there a developmental pathway in anthrobots for something analogous?
